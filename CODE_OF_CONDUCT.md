# Code of Conduct

FITPAC is intended to be a welcoming, rigorous community for people building, discussing, and improving an open standard for agentic software engineering.

This Code of Conduct applies to participation in this repository and related FITPAC community spaces, including issues, pull requests, discussions, documentation, and pattern submissions.

## Expected behavior

Participants are expected to:

- Treat others with respect and professionalism.
- Assume good faith where reasonable and seek clarification before escalating disagreements.
- Keep technical discussions constructive, evidence-based, and focused on the protocol, documentation, or implementation concerns at hand.
- Provide feedback that is specific, actionable, and relevant.
- Respect the distinction between normative standards, informative guidance, and personal opinion.
- Use private reporting paths for sensitive conduct or security concerns.

## Unacceptable behavior

Unacceptable behavior includes:

- Harassment, bullying, stalking, or intimidation.
- Discrimination or hateful conduct based on protected characteristics.
- Threats of violence or other harm.
- Doxxing or sharing private personal information without consent.
- Personal attacks, sustained hostility, or deliberately inflammatory behavior.
- Disruptive conduct that prevents others from participating effectively.
- Misrepresenting unofficial work as official FITPAC material.
- Retaliation against anyone who raises a concern in good faith.

## Reporting concerns

If you have a conduct concern, please report it privately to the maintainer.

Please include:

- a description of the behavior,
- links or screenshots if relevant,
- the date/time of the incident if known, and
- any other context that may help with review.

Do not use public issues for sensitive personal reports.

If the concern involves a security vulnerability, follow the repository’s security reporting path in `SECURITY.md` instead.

## Enforcement

The maintainer may take any action considered appropriate to protect the community and the integrity of the project, including:

- requesting a change in behavior,
- moderating, editing, locking, or removing content,
- closing discussions or pull requests,
- temporarily restricting participation, or
- permanently removing access for serious or repeated violations.

Enforcement decisions will be made in proportion to the severity and context of the conduct.

## Project status

FITPAC is currently maintained by Paul Roy. As project governance evolves, enforcement and reporting processes may be updated to reflect a broader maintainer or foundation model.
